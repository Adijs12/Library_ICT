Five Point Someone

Five Point Someone: What Not to Do at IIT" is a novel by Chetan Bhagat, published in 2004. The book is a humorous yet insightful look into the lives of three underperforming students at the prestigious Indian Institute of Technology (IIT). The story revolves around Hari, Ryan, and Alok, who struggle with the pressures of academic life and their personal dilemmas. The title "Five Point Someone" refers to their low grade point average (GPA) on a scale of ten, reflecting their academic struggles. The novel explores themes such as the flaws in the Indian education system, the importance of friendship, and the challenges of balancing personal dreams with societal expectations. "Five Point Someone" is well-known for its relatable characters, witty dialogues, and a narrative that blends humor with a critique of the high-pressure educational environment. It gained immense popularity in India, especially among young readers, and was later adapted into the successful Bollywood film "3 Idiots
